Bayesian Analysis of MLB Offensive Performance

In this project, I investigated the impact of the torpedo bat on key hitting metrics: batting average, on-base percentage, and slugging percentage.

Project Overview

At the time of the completion of the project, the torpedo bat was one of the most popular things to talk about in the world of baseball. Designed by Aaron Leanhardt, a former hitting coach for the New York Yankees with a PhD in physics, the torpedo bat was meant to be the answer to combat the rise in pitching quality over recent seasons. The bat he created, which breaks none of MLB's regulations on bats, redistributes weight to where a hitter prefers to make contact with the ball - known as the "barrel". This trend caught my attention as not only a fan of America's Pastime, but also as an analyst, due to the technical qualities that went into the design of the bat

Research Question

The question I ultimately set out to answer in this project was: "Did MLB hitters using torpedo bats exhibit meaningful improvements in offensive performance compared to previous seasons?"
